Below are some projects I did while studying at KTH.

Social feature for the Spotify app


Brief: The goal of the course was to learn user-centered design. We were given the task of looking at music consumption identify an interesting problem and turn it into a design opportunity. My group decided to explore the social aspect of music and more specifically how could Spotify connect people through its app.
Course: Interaction Design Methods (DH2628) at KTH.
Methods:focus group, interviews, bodystorming, user testing
Final deliverable: a hi-fi prototype of a feature which allows users to connect to people going to the same concert on the Spotify app.

Festagram: a social media app

Brief: The aim of the project was to find a way to reuse cultural heritage open data and crowdsourcing in an engaging way to solve the decreasing participation within the already existing social media tools of this kind.
Course: Social Media Technologies (DM2578) at KTH.
Methods:sketches, rapid prototyping, personas, user scenarios and interviews.
Final deliverable: A prototype which displays open data about festivities in different countries and from different times allowing users to contribute and interact with each other.

Digital bracelet: designing for relaxation

Brief: The aim of the project was to design a unique experience for one person. My partner wanted me to design something for relaxation.
Course: User Experience Design and Evaluation at KTH (DM2630).
Methods:interview, design probes, sketching (on paper & digital), storyboarding
Final deliverable: Physical design probes, a storyboard and digital sketches representing the idea.
